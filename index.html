Matthew Moore

Research

My doctoral research consisted of a series of investigations into human brain activity during social perception and facial expression processing using electroencephalography. I found suppression of the mu rhythm associated with social inferences and classification of emotions in facial expressions, but not during imitation of facial expressions. This is partially consistent with the mirror neuron model of social perception.

I have a general interest in overcoming technical challenges. I have taken advantage of my simultaneous awareness of the demands of research and the capabilities of technology innumerable times. If I am not familiar with a technology, I can readily acquire the skill (e.g. I didn't know how to make a website a month ago.)

I am currently employed at the University of Auckland's Centre for eResearch in Auckland, New Zealand. In this role I advise researchers on how they can leverage cutting-edge information technologies in the areas of advanced data visualisation, high-performance computing, and research data management. I am currently working on modeling the University of Auckland's data management planning needs ( Github link to work in progress).
